8 Signs It Is Time to Have Your Hearing Tested

By the team at Carolina Audiology Associates

Most adult hearing loss happens gradually, often over a decade or more. By the time it feels obvious, you have already missed years of clearer conversations. The good news is that the early signs are easy to spot once you know what to look for.

1. You ask people to repeat themselves more often

This is the most common first sign. If you find yourself saying "what?" or "say that again" several times a day, especially with women's and children's voices, your high-frequency hearing may be slipping.

2. The TV volume keeps climbing

If your family has commented that the television is louder than it used to be, that is meaningful data. The volume number does not lie.

3. Restaurants and crowded rooms wear you out

Background noise becomes exhausting when your brain has to work overtime to fill in the gaps. Listening fatigue is a real and well-documented symptom of hearing loss.

4. You miss the punchline at family gatherings

Group conversations, especially around a holiday table, can be the first place hearing loss shows up. You may nod along without actually catching what was said.

5. Phone calls have become harder

Phones strip out a surprising amount of acoustic information. If calls feel harder than in-person conversation, your hearing is doing more work than it should.

6. Voices sound mumbled, not quiet

People often describe early hearing loss as "everyone mumbles," not "everyone is too quiet." That is your high-frequency hearing dropping out, where consonants live.

7. You hear ringing or buzzing

Tinnitus often shows up alongside hearing loss. If you have ringing, buzzing, or hissing in your ears, even occasionally, it is worth a conversation.

8. Loved ones have mentioned it

Family members usually notice hearing loss before the person experiencing it does. If someone you trust has gently raised the topic, take it seriously.

What to do next

A comprehensive hearing evaluation gives you a clear baseline. At Carolina Audiology Associates, every evaluation is performed by a Doctor of Audiology and includes a full conversation about what you are noticing and what your options are. Schedule your consultation when you are ready.
