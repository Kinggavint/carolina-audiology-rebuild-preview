What to Expect at Your First Hearing Evaluation

By the team at Carolina Audiology Associates

If you have never had a hearing test before, the appointment can feel like a black box. It does not need to be. Here is what a comprehensive evaluation at Carolina Audiology Associates actually looks like, step by step.

1. The conversation comes first

Before any testing, we sit down and talk. We want to understand what you are noticing, when it started, what situations are hardest, and what you want to get back to. This conversation shapes everything that follows.

2. A quick health and history review

We review your medical history, any medications, exposure to loud noise, and family history of hearing loss. These details often point to the cause of what you are experiencing.

3. Otoscopy, a look in your ears

We examine your ear canals and eardrums with an otoscope. This rules out simple causes of muffled hearing, such as impacted earwax or signs of infection.

4. The hearing booth

You sit in a sound-treated booth, wear headphones, and respond to a series of tones at different pitches and volumes. This builds your audiogram, the map of your hearing thresholds.

5. Speech testing

Hearing tones is one thing. Understanding words is another. We test how well you can repeat words and sentences, in quiet and sometimes in background noise. Speech testing tells us how your brain is processing what your ears send up.

6. Middle-ear measurements

Tympanometry checks the movement of your eardrum and the health of your middle ear. It takes seconds and tells us a lot about what is happening behind the eardrum.

7. The results conversation

Your Doctor of Audiology walks you through the results in plain language. You see your audiogram, you understand what it means for daily life, and you learn what your options are. If you do not need hearing aids, we will tell you so.

How long does it take

Plan on about 60 to 75 minutes for a first evaluation. If you are bringing a family member, even better. Hearing loss affects the whole family, and we welcome them into the conversation.
