Hearing Aids

How to Care for Your Hearing Aids Day to Day

By the team at Carolina Audiology Associates

Modern hearing aids are remarkable pieces of technology, and they live in one of the harshest environments in the body. Wax, moisture, and motion are constant. A short daily routine extends the life of your devices and keeps them sounding right.

Wipe them down every night

Use a soft, dry cloth to wipe each hearing aid before you put it in its case. Skip wipes, sprays, alcohol, and water. Dry cleaning is best.

Check the wax filter and dome

Look at the part that sits in your ear canal. If you see wax buildup, change the wax filter or wipe the dome with a dry cloth. A clogged filter is the most common reason a hearing aid sounds quiet or distorted.

Store them in a dry place

A drying jar or electronic dehumidifier overnight pulls moisture out of the electronics. This single habit prevents a huge percentage of repairs.

Keep them out of the bathroom

Shower steam, hairspray, and lotions are tough on hearing aids. Put your aids in after you are dressed and groomed, not before.

Change batteries (or charge) on a schedule

If your aids use disposable batteries, change them on a predictable schedule so you are never caught with a dead aid mid-conversation. Rechargeable models should sit in their charger every night without exception.

Bring them in for service

Even with perfect home care, hearing aids benefit from a professional clean every few months. We do this in-office at Carolina Audiology Associates, and most cleanings take only a few minutes.

If your aids are not performing the way they used to, do not assume your hearing has changed. Bring them in for service first. Often a quick clean and filter change brings them right back.
